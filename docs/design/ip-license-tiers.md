## 0. Purpose

This document defines the **IP licensing scope** granted to brands that engage a LUMINA model. It is the consumer-facing contractual summary displayed at `/legal/ip-license`. The full contract is the engagement-specific agreement signed per client; this table binds LUMINA to not offer less than what is displayed.

## 2. What "use case" means per tier

### Standard
**Permitted**:
- Licensee's own e-commerce product pages (Rakuten / Yahoo Shopping / Amazon / Shopify / licensee-owned DTC site)
- Product thumbnails and gallery images for those pages
- Licensee's own email marketing (transactional order confirmations, abandonment recovery)

**Not permitted**:
- Any paid advertising
- Social media posts (licensee or third-party)
- Print, OOH, broadcast
- Editorial content (magazines, blogs beyond product catalogue)
- Wholesale/reseller distribution
- Sub-licensing to any third party

### Extended
**Adds to Standard**:
- Licensee's own SNS accounts (Instagram, X, TikTok, Facebook, LinkedIn, Pinterest, YouTube, other)
- Licensee's brand-owned blog, editorial, and content marketing
- Licensee's owned newsletter / email marketing (including promotional)
- 10 short videos per month (vertical 9:16, 16:9, or 1:1 up to 15 seconds)

**Not permitted**:
- Paid advertising (add Campaign tier)
- Sub-licensing
- Resellers/distributors using content independently

### Campaign
**Use cases**: Full above + paid advertising (Meta / Google / TikTok / X Ads / LINE Ads / YouTube Ads / OOH / print / broadcast / OTT), co-marketing with partner brands (within agreement scope), event and retail signage, PR/media distribution.

**Scope**: Negotiated per project. Script, shot-list, and final deliverables agreed in writing.

**Exclusivity**: During the active campaign window (e.g. 6-12 weeks), the named model will not appear in competing-category campaigns for other LUMINA clients. Outside the campaign window, the model returns to general availability unless upgraded to Exclusive.

**Political / religious / election-advocacy usage**: Requires additional Legal review and is NOT automatically included in Campaign tier.

### Exclusive
**Use cases**: All of Campaign, plus permanent category exclusivity.

**Category exclusivity definition**: For the duration of the contract, LUMINA will not license the named model to any other client whose business falls within the agreed-upon category (e.g. "women's sneakers" or "men's luxury watches"). Category scope is defined in writing at contract signing.

**Minimum term**: 6 months. Auto-renews in 6-month increments unless 90-day non-renewal notice given.

**Pricing**: From ¥200,000/month per model. Volume, category breadth, and media-spend commitments all affect final pricing. Enterprise / multi-model contracts negotiated separately.

## 3. Territory

- **Default**: Worldwide (Standard / Extended / Exclusive).
- **Campaign tier**: Defined per contract. Smaller territories may result in lower pricing.
- **Rights to distribute outside Japan** include all Berne Convention countries. Jurisdictions with unique name-and-likeness laws (e.g. California Civil Code §3344, Quebec equivalents) are honoured by LUMINA's prohibition on real-person impersonation (see AI Ethics Code §3).

| Tier | Minimum term | Default renewal | Cancellation notice |
|---|---|---|---|

| Extended | 1 month | Auto-renew monthly | 30 days before next cycle |

All tiers support **paid upgrade mid-term** (e.g. Standard → Extended) prorated to the remaining cycle.

## 5. What the licensee owns vs. what LUMINA owns

### LUMINA owns (perpetually, worldwide, exclusively)
- The model's name, identity, visual likeness, voice, and Character Bible
- Training pipelines and generation methodology
- Raw output generated before post-production by the licensee

### Licensee owns
- Their own garment designs, logos, brand assets, and brand copy
- Campaign-specific creative direction documents they author
- Finished composite content (e.g. edited videos, retouched images) — **but only the composite, not the underlying LUMINA model asset**

### Joint (requires mutual consent for further use)
- Co-created Campaign-tier concepts where LUMINA contributed creative direction
- Case study materials for LUMINA marketing (licensee consent required)

---

## 6. Content client supplies

- Garment photographs, technical specs, lookbooks, logos, and creative briefs remain fully owned by the licensee.
- LUMINA's license to reproduce those assets is **limited to the contracted engagement scope and duration**.
- Post-engagement: all client-supplied content is deleted within 90 days unless the client requests archival retention in writing (extends to 24 months max, then deleted).
- We do not train any model on client-supplied content (see AI Ethics Code §6).

---

## 7. Sub-licensing & transfer rules

- **No sub-licensing without written LUMINA consent.** Licensee cannot re-license the model to resellers, distributors, or partner brands.
- **Co-branded campaigns**: Permitted under Campaign/Exclusive tiers with explicit scope definition in the contract.
- **Agency arrangements**: If licensee is an agency acting on behalf of a named brand, the agency must disclose the end-client at contract signing. License is tied to the end-client, not the agency.
- **Acquisition/merger**: If licensee is acquired, the license survives for the remainder of the current term, then requires renewal in the new entity's name.

---

## 8. What gets revoked vs. what survives

### On non-renewal or cancellation
- **Revoked (cannot be used going forward)**:
  - Rights to generate new content featuring the model
  - Rights to new uses of existing content outside the original engagement scope
  - Right to claim current active partnership with the model
- **Survives (can continue to be used)**:
  - Content already published before termination, under the tier's original use-case scope
  - Historical marketing materials (editorial / archive / brand heritage)
  - Already-printed physical materials (no recall obligation)

### On model retirement by LUMINA (see Ethics Code §7)
- Licensees are notified 60 days in advance.
- No new content generation after retirement date.
- Existing deliverables remain licensed under original terms.
- Goodwill credits offered for substitution with a similar LUMINA model at current tier rate.

### On breach
- LUMINA reserves the right to terminate for cause with 14 days' notice for material breach (e.g. use outside tier scope, unauthorized sub-licensing, real-person impersonation attempt).
- Breach resolution preferred over termination; cure period typically offered unless the breach is irreversible.

---

## 9. Dispute resolution

- **Governing law**: Japan.
- **Forum**: Tokyo District Court (first instance), unless both parties agree to arbitration (Japan Commercial Arbitration Association).
- **Pre-litigation**: Mandatory 30-day good-faith negotiation period before any formal action.
- **Cross-border engagements**: Default governed by Japanese law; parties may agree to alternative jurisdiction in the engagement contract.

## 10. How this differs from traditional agency IP terms

| Aspect | Traditional modeling agency | **LUMINA** |
|---|---|---|
| Model is | A real person (human) | A fictional character (IP) |
| Who holds IP | Model + agency (split) | LUMINA (sole) |
| Real-person impersonation risk | Inherent (it IS a person) | Zero (explicitly prohibited — see Ethics §3) |
| Name/likeness rights | Complex, jurisdiction-dependent | Contractually simple — LUMINA controls |
| Release forms (撮影同意書) | Required per shoot | Not applicable (no human to release) |
| Residuals / ongoing royalties | Often applicable | Flat tier pricing, no residuals |
| Estate/posthumous rights | Relevant | Not applicable |

The simplicity above is **precisely what makes LUMINA's commercial proposition work** — and why careful IP governance (this document + Ethics Code) is the backbone of that simplicity.
